**Design Thinking Origins** (Phase 1, Topic 1 of 14)

## Summary

Tonight's research traced design thinking from its academic origins to mainstream business adoption. Key discoveries:

### Historical Arc
- **1969**: Herbert Simon's *Sciences of the Artificial* established the foundational 7-stage design process (define → research → ideate → prototype → choose → implement → learn)
- **1970s**: Horst Rittel introduced "wicked problems" — complex challenges with no single correct solution
- **1978**: IDEO founded, practicing human-centered design from day one
- **Late 1990s**: IDEO's DeepDive™ methodology featured on ABC Nightline, popularizing rapid innovation
- **2003**: Stanford d.school founded, codifying the 5-stage model (empathize → define → ideate → prototype → test)

### Key Insight
The term "design thinking" was coined to describe **the learnable and teachable elements** of design practice: empathy, optimism, iteration, creative confidence, experimentation, and an embrace of ambiguity. IDEO's philosophy: "Give someone a fish, and they'll have food for a day; teach someone to fish, and they'll have food for life."

### Notable Finding
Design thinking is explicitly about **wicked problems** — those that are:
- Ill-defined with no clear formulation
- Have no right/wrong answer
- Involve conflicting stakeholder perspectives
- Can't be solved with technical knowledge alone

This distinguishes it from traditional problem-solving and connects directly to the service design and business architecture challenges we'll explore in later research.
