## Summary

Researched DesignOps — the operational discipline that enables design to scale while maintaining quality, consistency, and strategic impact. This sits at the intersection of service design and enterprise architecture, bridging customer experience intent with systematic execution.

## Key Findings

### Definition & Purpose

DesignOps is "the orchestration and optimization of people, processes, and craft to amplify design's value and impact at scale" (Nielsen Norman Group). It addresses the operational challenges of growing design teams: hiring, workflows, quality control, and scaling impact.

**Why now?** Three critical shifts:
1. Design has "won a seat at the table" — but strategic involvement means designers are often too busy to actually design
2. Increasing complexity — products are more elaborate, teams are distributed
3. Embedded team models create designer silos and fragmented practice

### Core Frameworks

**Nielsen Norman Group's Three-Area Model** (Kate Kaplan):
- **How We Work Together**: Organize, Collaborate, Humanize
- **How We Get Our Work Done**: Standardize, Harmonize, Prioritize
- **How Our Work Creates Impact**: Measure, Socialize, Enable

**Simon James Ward's Four Sub-Functions** (treating DesignOps as infrastructure):
1. **Design Governance** — Decision-making and standards
2. **Design Infrastructure** — Technical foundation (systems, tokens, integrations)
3. **Design Intelligence** — Metrics, KPIs, impact measurement
4. **Design Enablement** — Culture, training, community

Central role: **DesignOps Product Owner** manages these four sub-functions as a coherent operating model.

### Design System Governance

**Brad Frost's 10-Step Process** (building on Inayaili de León Persson's Canonical work):
- From "I need a component" to "component is live in production"
- Key decision: Snowflake (one-off) vs. System component (general-use)
- Comprehensive testing: content, accessibility, cross-browser, responsive, functionality
- Clear release cadence using semantic versioning

**Three governance models**:
- Top-down centralized (dedicated DS team)
- Federated (DS team sets standards, product teams contribute)
- Distributed (product teams own with loose coordination)

### Maturity & Current State

**NN/G 2022 Research** (557 practitioners):
- Only **22%** of recommended DesignOps efforts are implemented
- **Low maturity overall** — most organizations lack dedicated DesignOps roles
- **Designer-to-operator ratio**: 25:1

**Trend**: Organizations increasingly invest in DesignOps *early* (when teams are small) to prevent operational debt accumulation.

### Measuring Success

Five key metrics (Simon James Ward):
1. **Efficiency** — Reduced duplication, faster cycles
2. **Quality** — Consistent accessibility and usability
3. **Adoption** — Higher component reuse
4. **Impact** — Linkage between design maturity and CX outcomes
5. **Culture** — Improved collaboration and wellbeing

### Connection to CX & Enterprise Architecture

DesignOps sits between **CX Strategy** (what customers should feel) and **Product Delivery** (what gets built). It's the operational bridge translating customer experience intent into systematic execution.

**Strategic insight**: "DesignOps is not an administrative layer. It is the operating system which makes design scalable, measurable, and strategically relevant." — Simon James Ward

Design systems map to **business capabilities** in EA. DesignOps ensures design components align with capability models, decisions trace to strategic objectives, and design quality feeds capability maturity assessments.
