## Key Findings

### Core Concept

Business capability mapping creates a **stable, technology-agnostic view** of organizational abilities. Unlike processes (which change frequently) or org charts (which restructure), capabilities represent enduring "what we do" abstractions that:

- Remain stable over time
- Focus on outcomes, not implementation
- Provide a common language between business and IT
- Enable strategic planning and investment decisions

### Hierarchy Best Practices

Research confirmed that **2-3 levels maximum** is the sweet spot:
1. **Level 1:** ~10 top-level capabilities (major domains)
2. **Level 2:** Sub-capabilities
3. **Level 3:** Detailed capabilities (use sparingly)

Over-decomposition creates complexity without value.

### Three Critical Assessment Dimensions

1. **Strategic Importance** — Which capabilities are required to deliver strategy?
2. **Maturity** — Which capabilities are performing below expectations?
3. **Adaptability** — Which capabilities are difficult to change or scale?

### Heat Mapping as Decision Tool

Heat maps emerged as the killer visualization technique:
- **Maturity heat maps:** Green (target), yellow (one level below), red (two+ levels below)
- **Strategic importance maps:** Guide investment prioritization
- **Risk maps:** Identify dependencies on underperforming tech
- **M&A maps:** Compare capabilities across merging organizations

Heat maps transform assessment data into executive-friendly visuals that drive decisions.

### Capability-Based Planning (CBP) Cycle

**MAP → ASSESS → PLAN → CONTROL**

This cycle connects capability mapping to strategic portfolio management:
- Every initiative should map to capabilities
- Investments prioritized by strategic importance + maturity gaps
- Scenario planning enabled through capability modeling
- ROI measured in capability maturity improvements

This research highlights a critical convergence point:

**Service Design (outside-in)** ← → **Enterprise Architecture (inside-out)**

Capability mapping increasingly connects to service design through:
- Service capabilities (abilities to deliver/support services)
