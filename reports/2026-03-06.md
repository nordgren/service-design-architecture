## Research Summary

Tonight's research focused on how organizations successfully adopt journey-centric operating models—one of the most significant transformations in modern customer experience management. This represents a fundamental shift from product-centric to journey-centric operations, requiring changes in mindset, structure, governance, and culture across entire enterprises.

### Key Frameworks Discovered

#### 1. Forrester's Three-Phase Transformation Framework

Forrester Research has established a comprehensive model for journey-centric transformation with three phases and six operational levers:

**Three Phases:**
- **Activate:** Build momentum with executive sponsors, lighthouse journeys, and pilot teams (6-12 months)
- **Connect:** Establish executive alignment, universal frameworks, and standardized practices (12-24 months)
- **Extend:** Scale with senior-level prioritization forums, permanent journey teams, and advanced analytics (ongoing)

**Six Operational Levers** (must move in concert):
1. Strategy & Vision
2. Structure (teams and roles)
3. Process (playbooks and methodologies)
4. Technology (analytics and orchestration)
5. Governance (decision rights and forums)
6. Measurement (KPIs and dashboards)

A critical insight: Organizations often have imbalances where some levers advance faster than others (e.g., strong teams but weak technology, or journey-mapping software without teams to operationalize insights).

#### 2. TheyDo's Five-Stage Maturity Model

TheyDo developed a maturity model based on interviews with hundreds of organizations:

**Five Maturity Levels:**
1. **Intuition** - Isolated innovators, no systematic approach
2. **Fragmented** - Sporadic initiatives in silos
3. **Coordinated** - Cross-functional collaboration emerging, formalized budgets
4. **Scalable** - Dedicated teams, standardized processes, feedback flywheels
5. **Excellence** - Customer-centricity in organizational fabric, strategy directly informed by customers

**Six Assessment Dimensions:**
- Governance
- Process
- Culture
- Organization
- Measurement
- Tools

#### 3. Nielsen Norman Group's Journey Management Lessons

NN/g conducted in-depth research with four large organizations (Jumbo Supermarkets, PostNL, and two anonymous firms) to extract lessons learned. Key findings:

**Three Pillars of Success:**
- **Experimentation and Iteration** - Trial and error with institutional tolerance
- **Consistency** - Standardized practices with room for situational adaptation
- **Patience** - Recognition that transformation is never "done"

### Critical Success Factors

1. **Change Mindset Before Operations:** Organizations must create shared understanding and buy-in before deliverables. This requires training, time (sometimes several months), and socialization through case studies.

2. **Top-Down + Bottom-Up:** A C-suite mandate isn't enough. Transformation requires buy-in across the organization at all levels.

3. **The Mantra: Cooperate, Don't Compete:** Journey-centric design demands cross-functional collaboration. Organizations must create business-driven priorities that reduce competition between departments.

4. **Balance Autonomy and Control:** Successful organizations create journey management playbooks for governance while supporting trial and error. Some establish dedicated "journey operations teams" to onboard new journey teams and adjust processes.

5. **Relate Everything to Business Goals:** The financial institution case study showed how quantifying unrealized gains and projecting revenue improvements doubled tool adoption and reduced dropoff by 12%.

### Innovative Approaches

**Stakeholder Journey Mapping:** Forward-thinking organizations are applying journey mapping not just to customers, but to:
- Employee journeys
- Partner journeys
- Supplier journeys
- Change management stakeholder journeys

This approach helps identify friction points in transformation itself and accelerates adoption.

**Flexible Planning Framework:** Leading organizations use tiered certainty in roadmaps:
- Next quarter: High certainty, firm plans
- Following quarters: Medium certainty, flexible
- Beyond 6 months: Loose placeholders, strategic themes

### Common Pitfalls Identified

1. Tools before teams (investing in technology before organizational readiness)
2. Rushing the mindset shift
3. Top-down mandate only
4. Allowing journey and product teams to compete for resources
5. Lack of governance and playbooks
6. Short-term thinking (expecting quick results from multi-year transformation)
7. Treating transformation as a project with an end date

### Organizations Leading the Way

- **Jumbo Supermarkets** (Netherlands, 100K employees) - Conquered fragmentation with journey-driven workflow
- **PostNL** (35K employees) - Parcel and ecommerce journey management
- **Anonymous Financial Services** (58K employees) - Investment journey redesign doubled tool adoption
- **NCR** - Journey management at scale
- **Bol.com** - Journey approach for 36,000 partners
