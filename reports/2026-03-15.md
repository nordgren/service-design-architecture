# Nightly Research Report: 2026-03-15

## Topic Completed
**AI in Journey Orchestration** (Topic 20, Phase 5: Emerging Topics)

## Summary

Tonight's research explored the transformation of customer journey orchestration through AI, focusing on how autonomous agents, real-time decisioning engines, and predictive personalization are replacing static, rule-based workflows.

### Key Findings

**1. Fundamental Shift in Journey Design**
- Moving from linear funnels to adaptive, customer-led experiences
- 95% of customer interactions projected to be AI-handled by 2025
- Journeys are now co-created with customers as "co-pilots" rather than passive participants
- Real-time adaptation based on behavioral signals, sentiment, and context

**2. Agentic AI Architecture**
The research uncovered a clear distinction between AI agents and AI orchestration:
- **AI agents** = specialized, autonomous entities (intent detection, knowledge retrieval, recommendations, sentiment analysis)
- **AI orchestration** = coordinating intelligence that decides which agent acts, when, and why
- Multiple agents work together under unified conversation state, enabling complex problem-solving beyond single-model capabilities

**3. Leading Platform Capabilities**

**Adobe Journey Optimizer** (most comprehensive):
- Journey Agent: diagnoses performance gaps, identifies friction points
- Experimentation Agent: accelerates test-and-learn cycles
- Customer AI: predictive scoring for churn, conversion, affinity
- Real-time decisioning with Bayesian/Thompson sampling
- Content Assistant (Firefly + Azure OpenAI) for scaled content generation
- Intelligent offer catalog with AI governance (frequency caps, fatigue prevention)

**Infobip AgentOS** (launched 2026):
- Orchestration-first architecture built on billions of annual interactions
- Autonomous agents + Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) model
- Conversational CDP for real-time context
- Visual builder with multi-LLM support
- Bridges IVR, AI agents, and human teams

**Other notable platforms:**
- UJET AXO (March 2026): Agentic Experience Orchestration with Computer-Using Agents
- Salesforce Einstein: Decision engine augmenting existing journeys
- Genesys: Agentic AI aligned to business intent

**4. Core Orchestration Capabilities**
- **Real-time decisioning**: Predictive analytics, sentiment analysis, reinforcement learning
- **Conversational CDP integration**: Unified profiles with full conversation history
- **Human-in-the-loop**: Seamless handoff with full context, AI stays engaged to learn
- **Omnichannel continuity**: AR/VR, voice, messaging, IoT, physical stores
- **Predictive personalization**: Anticipatory recommendations before customers articulate needs

**5. Business Impact**
- 71% of consumers expect personalized interactions (McKinsey)
- Higher conversion efficiency, stronger loyalty, reduced operational load
- Scalability: dynamic resource allocation without manual reconfiguration
- Better governance through centralized monitoring and compliance

**6. Implementation Strategy**
Clear roadmap emerged from research:
1. Audit journeys and data availability
2. Prioritize high-friction use cases
3. Apply predictive segmentation
4. Test continuously (AI-driven vs. rules-based)
5. Strengthen data connections (CDP, CRM integration)
6. Build for orchestration, not silos

**7. Emerging Trends (2025-2026)**
- **Multi-experience orchestration**: AI coordinating across multiple senses and modes (Gartner)
- **Zero-friction experiences**: AI handles authentication, forms, support automatically
- **AI-driven creative production**: Dynamic content assembly, virtual influencers
- **Data embedded in workflows**: Real-time intelligence, not separate analytics cycles
- **Agentic workforce models**: AI as coworker with defined roles, not just tool

## Critical Insights for Mattias

**Strategic Implications:**
1. **Journey orchestration is becoming infrastructure** - Like CDPs before them, orchestration platforms will become foundational to enterprise CX stacks. Early adoption = competitive advantage.

2. **The "agentic workforce" model is real** - Not hype. Multiple agents with specialized roles, coordinated by orchestration layer, is the pattern that's winning. Infobip's architecture validates this approach.

3. **Human-AI collaboration > pure automation** - The HITL model where AI stays engaged during human interactions (to learn and assist) is the sophisticated approach. Pure replacement strategies miss the point.

4. **Data quality is the bottleneck** - All the AI capability in the world can't overcome fragmented customer data. CDP + real-time pipelines + identity resolution are table stakes.

5. **Measurement must shift** - Journey-level KPIs, not channel metrics. Customer lifetime value impact, not campaign CTR. AI decision quality, not just efficiency gains.

**Practitioner Landscape:**
- Adobe dominates the martech conversation (6 distinct AI capabilities in AJO)
- Infobip represents the "orchestration-first" new entrants challenging incumbents
- UJET's "Computer-Using Agents" (March 2026) signals next evolution: AI that can operate enterprise software directly
- Salesforce playing catch-up with Einstein as retrofit vs. Adobe's native integration

**Caution Flags:**
- Ethics and transparency challenges mounting (bias in predictive models, consent, customer control)
- Over-automation risk: losing human touch in critical moments
- Organizational change is harder than tech: legacy mindsets, silos, skills gaps
- Privacy regulations likely to tighten as AI orchestration scales
