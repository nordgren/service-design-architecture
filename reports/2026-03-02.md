## Research Summary

Tonight's research focused on **Journey Performance Management**, exploring how organizations measure and optimize customer experiences at the journey level rather than at isolated touchpoints. This represents a critical shift in CX measurement philosophy and practice.

## Key Findings

### 1. Journey vs. Touchpoint Measurement Paradigm
The fundamental insight: customers can rate every individual touchpoint highly yet still find the overall journey frustrating. Journey metrics measure end-to-end experiences against customer goals, revealing systemic issues that touchpoint metrics miss. This distinction changes what you measure, how you diagnose problems, and where interventions create impact.

### 2. The Three-Category Framework
Journey metrics organize into three essential categories:
- **Perception metrics** (NPS, CSAT, CES): How customers feel
- **Outcome metrics** (conversion, completion, FCR): What actually happened
- **Business impact metrics** (CLV, churn, cost to serve): What it means for the organization

Best practice: Start with 3 metrics per journey (one from each category), expanding only when diagnostic depth is needed.

### 3. Leading vs. Lagging Indicator Pairing
Critical strategic distinction often missing from guidance:
- **Lagging indicators** confirm what already happened (damage done)
- **Leading indicators** signal what's coming (time to prevent)

Example pairing: CES increase (leading) predicts NPS decline (lagging). Measurement systems with only lagging indicators are purely reactive; those with leading indicators can prevent problems.

### 4. The Detect-Diagnose-Respond Framework
Smaply's actionable methodology for turning metrics into decisions:
- **Detect:** Set thresholds distinguishing signal from noise (absolute, trend, comparative)
- **Diagnose:** Drill into journey maps, cross-reference metrics, check leading indicators, talk to customers
- **Respond:** Prioritize by impact, connect to roadmap, track interventions via leading indicators first

### 5. Metrics Overload Problem
Gartner research reveals large organizations track 50-200+ CX metrics, creating overwhelming overhead without necessarily improving outcomes. MIT Sloan research (Feb 2026) shows companies conducting metric rationalization programs—eliminating low-value metrics and focusing on fewer, better-aligned measures—yield tracking efficiencies and more actionable insights.

### 6. Map-Metric Integration
Metrics in dashboards show numbers; metrics on journey maps show context. The difference is diagnostic power. Strategic placement:
- Perception metrics at collection stages
- Outcome metrics at transition points
- Business impact metrics at journey level

Live data feeds keep maps current and trigger reviews when performance shifts.

### 7. Cross-Silo Measurement Challenges
Journey performance management inherently requires cross-functional measurement because journeys span organizational boundaries. Key challenges: data silos, misaligned incentives (local KPI optimization hurting journey outcomes), attribution complexity, governance gaps. Requires dedicated journey ownership (covered in previous topic).

### 8. Common Mistakes Documented
Six critical antipatterns identified:
1. Measuring everything, acting on nothing
2. Touchpoint scores without journey context
3. Lagging indicators only
4. Metrics disconnected from maps
5. No baseline or trend tracking
6. Vanity metrics that don't inform decisions

### 9. Emerging Practices (2025-2026)
- AI-powered journey analytics (automated text/speech analysis at scale)
- Predictive journey health scoring (ML models enabling preemptive intervention)
- Journey orchestration integration (real-time measurement feeding automated adjustments)
- Formal metric rationalization programs in large enterprises

### 10. Journey-Specific Metric Selection
Not all journeys need same metrics. Different priorities by type:
- Acquisition/Conversion → conversion rate, stage drop-off, cost
- Onboarding → time to value, completion, CES
- Service/Support → FCR, CES, CSAT
- Retention/Renewal → churn, CLV, NPS
- Advocacy → NPS, referral rate

## Concepts Worth Highlighting for Mattias

1. **The metrics overload problem** is real and documented—enterprises tracking 100+ metrics with diminishing returns. The discipline of selecting fewer, better metrics with clear action frameworks is strategic differentiator.

2. **Leading indicator thinking** separates reactive from preventive organizations. This applies beyond CX to any performance management context.

3. **The Detect-Diagnose-Respond framework** is clean, actionable, and missing from most enterprise practices that collect metrics without systematic response processes.

4. **Map-metric integration** provides the spatial context that enables diagnosis—pure dashboard measurement lacks this diagnostic power.

5. **Cross-silo measurement** inherently requires new governance models (journey ownership) because traditional functional KPIs can optimize locally while hurting journey-level outcomes.
