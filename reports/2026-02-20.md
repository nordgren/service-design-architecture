## Research Summary

Tonight's research completed Phase 1 foundational knowledge on Enterprise Architecture, specifically covering TOGAF, Zachman Framework, and the four core EA domains.

### Key Findings

#### TOGAF Framework
- **Core insight**: The ADM is an **information flow model**, not a linear waterfall—inherently iterative and incremental
- **Nine ADM phases** plus continuous Requirements Management
- **Four architecture domains**: Business, Information Systems (Data + Applications), Technology, and Architecture Vision
- **Modern interpretation**: Best practice emphasizes consuming existing EA repository knowledge and focusing on gaps

#### Zachman Framework
- **Fundamental distinction**: Ontology (structure) vs. Methodology (process)
- **6×6 matrix**: 36 cells mapping interrogatives (What/How/Where/Who/When/Why) against perspectives (Planner/Owner/Designer/Builder/Subcontractor/Enterprise)
- **Primitive vs. Composite**: Zachman provides the timeless elements; methodologies create temporal implementations
- **Created 1987** by John Zachman—watershed moment in EA history

#### The Four EA Domains
Comprehensive coverage of:
1. **Business Architecture** — Strategy, capabilities, processes, organization
2. **Information Architecture** — Data models, governance, metadata, BI
3. **Application Architecture** — Portfolio management, SOA, integration, UX
4. **Technology Architecture** — Infrastructure, standards, security, cloud

#### Other Frameworks
- **ArchiMate**: Modeling language, often paired with TOGAF
- **FEAF**: Federal government-focused, limited adoption outside
- **DoDAF**: Defense-specific, sophisticated but specialized

### Key Concepts Captured
- Domain layering (Environment → Business → Data → Information Systems → Technology)
- Tools and techniques for each domain (BPM, VSM, ERDs, UML, IaC)
- Principle of alignment: IT strategy must serve business strategy
- Importance of stakeholder-specific views
