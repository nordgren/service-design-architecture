## Topic Researched
**Value Stream Design** — Intersections of service design, lean thinking, and business architecture

---

## Summary

Tonight's research explored value stream design as a critical convergence point where service design principles meet lean operational thinking and business architecture practices. The topic revealed multiple perspectives on value streams—from SAFe's operational vs. development streams to TOGAF's strategic business architecture view to Lean's waste-elimination focus.

**Key insight:** Value streams provide the operational backbone for customer experience design. While journey maps capture the experiential perspective, value streams capture the delivery perspective. The sweet spot lies in overlaying these to reveal where operational pain creates experience friction.

---

## Key Concepts Documented

### 1. Types of Value Streams
- **Operational Value Streams (OVS)**: Direct customer value delivery (e.g., Order to Cash, Prospect to Customer)
- **Development Value Streams (DVS)**: Building the solutions that enable OVS (e.g., Concept to Realization)
- **Lean Value Stream Mapping**: Analytical technique for waste elimination and flow improvement

### 2. Cross-Mapping Power
The real value emerges from mapping value streams to:
- **Capabilities** (what the business can do)
- **Processes** (detailed execution steps)
- **Applications/IT Services** (technology support)
- **Customer Journeys** (experience perspective)
- **Service Blueprints** (frontstage/backstage orchestration)

Classic framing: *"Capabilities visualize the business at rest; Value Streams visualize the business in motion."*

### 3. Service Design Integration Patterns
- **Journey-Driven Value Stream Redesign**: Start with experience gaps, redesign operations
- **Value Stream as Blueprint Backbone**: Use stream stages as blueprint structure
- **Capability-Led Experience Design**: Prioritize capability investment based on experience impact
