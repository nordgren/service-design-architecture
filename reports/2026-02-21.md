## Research Summary

Tonight's research explored **Customer Journey Mapping**, marking the beginning of Phase 2 (Methods) in the knowledge base. This is a pivotal shift from foundational concepts to practical techniques that connect service design with business architecture.

### Key Findings

**1. Paradigm Shift in Journey Mapping (2024-2025)**
Journey mapping has evolved dramatically from static, one-off diagrams into dynamic, AI-powered orchestration systems. Modern approaches emphasize:
- Real-time analytics and continuous optimization
- Direct connection to business metrics (CLV, retention, NPS)
- Cross-functional operational tools that break down silos
- Behavioral specificity over broad journey stages

**2. Service Safari Methodology**
Discovered rich detail on **service safaris** as an immersive ethnographic research method:
- Researchers experience services as customers do, in context and in-the-moment
- More than just observation—builds empathy through direct engagement
- Can be conducted for both physical and digital services
- Often the first research method used in the empathize stage of design thinking
- Informs future research, journey maps, and service blueprints

**3. Moments of Truth**
The concept of "moments of truth"—critical touchpoints that significantly shape customer perception—emerged as central to modern journey mapping. Originally from Jan Carlzon (SAS Airlines, 1980s), now foundational to identifying high-impact intervention points.

**4. Emotional Intelligence in Mapping**
Recent data (Salesforce 2024, PwC) confirms that emotional connection is as important as functional performance:
- 80% of consumers say experience is as important as products/services
- 65% are more loyal to brands creating positive experiences
- Modern maps must capture emotions, not just actions

**5. Real-World Innovation**
Documented three compelling case studies:
- **LEGO's Experience Wheel** — Visualizes before/during/after phases with emotion tracking
- **American Express** — AI-powered sentiment modeling replaces transactional surveys
- **IKEA** — Masters physical-digital integration through context-aware journey design

### Frameworks & Methodologies

**Core Components:**
1. Personas (data-driven customer archetypes)
2. Actions (behavioral patterns and decision points)
3. Timeline (engagement rhythms)
4. Channels (omnichannel touchpoint mapping)
5. Emotions, Expectations & Questions (the "why" behind behavior)

**The Modern 8-Step Process:**
Research → Build Personas → Identify Pain Points → Map Touchpoints → Optimize Interactions → Find Insights → Revise → Instrument/Orchestrate/Test

**Best Practices Framework (Three Ps):**
- Practice: Start with customer perspective, break down silos, use multichannel data, map emotions, iterate continuously
- Purpose: Each practice tied to specific outcomes
- Practical Tips: Actionable guidance for implementation

## Synthesis & Insights

### Emerging Themes
1. **AI as transformation agent** — Not just automation, but sentiment analysis, predictive intelligence, real-time orchestration
2. **Emotion > Function** — Customer loyalty driven more by how experiences make people feel than by feature sets
3. **Living systems over static artifacts** — Continuous measurement, orchestration, and iteration now table stakes
4. **Behavioral granularity** — Modern maps track micro-behaviors (browsed but didn't add to cart) not just macro stages

### Strategic Implications for Service Design × Business Architecture
Journey mapping is the **connective tissue** between:
- Service design's human-centered approach
- Business architecture's capability and value stream modeling
- Enterprise architecture's systems thinking

Organizations that excel at journey mapping:
- Break down internal silos
- Ground decisions in customer reality (not assumptions)
- Measure what matters (business outcomes, not vanity metrics)
- Adapt in real-time to changing customer needs
