## Summary

Tonight's research focused on the foundations of Business Architecture, particularly the BIZBOK® framework maintained by the Business Architecture Guild. This is a critical area where Service Design and Enterprise Architecture intersect—BA provides the strategic business context layer that both disciplines build upon.

### Key Findings

**1. BIZBOK® Framework (Business Architecture Body of Knowledge)**
- The definitive industry standard for business architecture practice
- Maintained by Business Architecture Guild
- Version 10.0+ with regular updates
- Industry-specific reference models available (~$150/year membership)

**2. Four Core Domains of Business Architecture**
- **Value Streams:** End-to-end value delivery flows (customer-initiated)
- **Capabilities:** What the business can do (technology-agnostic, stable)
- **Information:** Business-level data concepts and flows
- **Organization:** Structure, roles, accountabilities

**3. Critical Relationships**
- **Value Stream Stages ↔ Capabilities:** The cross-mapping blueprint is one of the most essential BA artifacts
- **Capabilities ≠ Processes:** Capabilities define *what* (strategic, stable); processes define *how* (operational, changes frequently)
- Business architecture views value stream stages as *collections of capabilities*

**4. Capability Mapping Best Practices**
- Keep hierarchy 2-3 levels deep (avoid over-complication)
- Distinguish core vs. non-core capabilities (outsourcing decisions)
- Use heat maps for maturity visualization
- One capability map per organization (foundational principle)

**5. Integration with Enterprise Architecture**
- BA provides the *business layer* for EA frameworks like TOGAF
- TOGAF 10 incorporated BIZBOK-aligned concepts
- ArchiMate® modeling language maps to BIZBOK® metamodel
- BA is technology-agnostic; EA adds application/infrastructure layers

**6. Practical Applications**
- Strategic planning and alignment
- Operating model design
- Digital transformation roadmaps
- M&A integration planning
- Make-vs-buy and outsourcing decisions

> — BIZBOK® Guide

> "Value streams are not capabilities, nor components of capabilities... they specifically describe the sequence of activities that the business needs to undertake to produce or realize value."

- Dependencies and interrelationships across the business

## Connections to Service Design

**Emerging patterns:**

1. **Service Blueprinting ↔ Value Streams:**
   - Both map end-to-end flows
   - Blueprints show *who does what* (operational)
   - Value streams show *value creation sequence* (strategic)
   - Potential synthesis: map blueprints to value stream stages?

2. **Capabilities as Design Constraints:**
   - Service designers need to understand organizational capability maturity
   - Capability gaps inform service design feasibility
   - Capability heat maps can guide service innovation priorities

3. **Customer Journey ↔ Value Streams:**
   - Journey maps capture experience touchpoints
   - Value streams capture business activities delivering that experience
   - Overlay provides powerful alignment view

4. **Journey Ops as BA Practice:**
   - Journey operations is essentially applying BA principles to CX
   - Journey mapping → value stream design
   - Touchpoint optimization → capability development
