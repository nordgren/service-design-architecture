## Research Summary

Tonight's research focused on **Wardley Mapping** — a strategic visualization technique that has become increasingly influential in service design, business architecture, and technology strategy over the past decade.

### What is Wardley Mapping?

Wardley Mapping is a method for creating "topographical" maps of business strategy by plotting components on two axes:
- **Y-axis (vertical):** Value chain position / visibility to user
- **X-axis (horizontal):** Evolutionary stage (Genesis → Custom → Product → Commodity)

This creates situational awareness — making the invisible visible and enabling strategic conversations grounded in visual, challengeable models.

### Key Historical Points

- **Created by Simon Wardley** at Fotango in 2005 (evolutionary framing developed 2004)
- **Further developed** at Canonical UK (2008-2010)
- **Published openly** in 2017-2018 via Medium (19-chapter series, now compiled as free ebook)
- **Licensed CC BY-SA 4.0** — freely available for anyone to learn and use
- **Wide adoption** in UK Government Digital Service (GDS) for digital transformation and strategic planning

### Core Concepts Captured

1. **Everything Evolves:** The fundamental premise that all capabilities, technologies, and practices move from novel (genesis) to commodity through supply-demand competition

2. **Four Evolutionary Stages:**
   - Genesis (novel, uncertain, high-risk)
   - Custom (emerging, bespoke)
   - Product (stabilizing, off-the-shelf)
   - Commodity (mature, standardized, utility)

3. **The Strategy Cycle:** Integration of Sun Tzu's five factors (Purpose, Landscape, Climate, Doctrine, Leadership) with Boyd's OODA loop (Observe, Orient, Decide, Act)

4. **Climatic Patterns:** Predictable forces that drive component evolution

5. **Forms of Gameplay:** 61+ strategic "moves" including exploiting evolution, creating barriers, accelerating commoditization

### Integration with Service Design & Business Architecture

This research highlighted several critical intersections:

**Service Design:**
- Complements journey mapping (showing the *system* beneath the *sequence*)
- Informs service blueprinting (adding evolutionary dimension to frontstage/backstage)
- Enables strategic service innovation by leveraging commoditizing infrastructure

**Business Architecture:**
- Enhances capability mapping (adding "how evolved" to "what exists")
- Provides evolutionary context for value streams
- Bridges business strategy and technical architecture
- Informs build/buy/partner decisions based on evolutionary positioning

**Architecture for Flow:**
Recent work (Susanne Kaiser, 2025) explicitly combines Wardley Mapping + Domain-Driven Design + Team Topologies to create adaptive socio-technical systems — directly relevant to enterprise transformation.

### Practical Applications Documented

- Strategic planning and continuous strategy cycles
- Build vs. buy decisions based on evolutionary stage
- Investment allocation across portfolios
- Organizational design (different cultures for different evolutionary stages)
- Risk assessment (execution risk vs. commoditization risk)
- Government digital service modernization (UK GDS case studies)

### Critical Perspectives

Balanced view including:
- **Strengths:** Visual, tangible, forces explicit assumptions, reveals dependencies
- **Critiques:** Subjectivity in positioning, risk of "assumption laundering," learning curve
- **Best practices:** Treat maps as hypotheses, map collaboratively, iterate frequently
