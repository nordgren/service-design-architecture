# Research Report: 2026-02-25

## Topic Researched
**CX meets EA** - Customer Experience × Enterprise Architecture Integration

## Phase
Phase 3: Intersections - Experience Architecture

## Summary

Tonight's research explored the critical intersection between Customer Experience (CX) design and Enterprise Architecture (EA), investigating how these historically separate disciplines are converging to enable customer-centric transformation. The research reveals a mature body of practice with established frameworks, clear integration patterns, and growing recognition that neither discipline can succeed in isolation.

## Key Findings

### 1. The Integration Imperative
CX and EA represent a symbiotic relationship where each discipline amplifies the other:
- **CX provides:** Customer vision, journey maps, touchpoint design, and priority focus areas
- **EA provides:** Translation layer from vision to reality, capability structures, system architecture, and initiative coordination
- **Together they enable:** Strategy-to-execution flow that transforms customer needs into operational reality

### 2. Service Architecture as Unifying Scaffold
One of the most powerful concepts discovered is Livework Studio's **Service Architecture** - a single, shared view of how organizations deliver customer value:
- Uses services as stable anchoring point (changes less than org structure, tech, or products)
- Provides common reference for all organizational views to connect
- Enables layered analysis (like Google Maps) with multiple overlays for strategic insight
- Once seen, fundamentally changes how organizations view themselves

### 3. Customer Experience Capabilities Model
Perficient's structured approach organizes CX delivery around:
- Customer at center
- Channel/device layer
- Customer acquisition lifecycle
- Departmental ownership ring
- Specific capabilities within each area

This model serves multiple purposes: gap analysis, redundancy elimination, vendor evaluation, and dependency mapping.

### 4. Cross-Mapping as Core Pattern
The fundamental integration mechanism is **direct mapping between:**
- Customer journey phases → Value stream stages → Business capabilities

This creates traceability from customer needs through operational delivery, enabling:
- Impact analysis when changes occur
- Validation that architecture supports desired experiences
- Strategic decision-making with full context

### 5. Five-Stage Strategy Execution Model
Research uncovered clear end-to-end process when CX and EA collaborate:
1. Strategy formulation (both teams inform/consume)
2. Experience design (CX team, informed by EA current state)
3. Translation (EA converts designs to capability/system changes)
4. Initiative planning (changes packaged into coordinated initiatives)
5. Execution & measurement (funded initiatives execute with metrics)

### 6. Governance Structures
Successful integration requires:
- Leadership groups serving as decision makers (beyond just CX team)
- Clear decision rights between CX design and architecture implementation
- Capability-based governance models
- Cross-functional coordination mechanisms

### 7. Technology Architecture Evolution
Modern CX architectures are shifting:
- **From:** Centralized CDPs as single source of truth
- **To:** Composable, decentralized, event-driven systems
- **Enabling:** Real-time personalization, cloud-native microservices, domain-driven data ownership
- **Components:** Unified customer data, omnichannel integration, feedback loops, AI/personalization, content management, analytics

## Critical Success Factors

For successful CX-EA integration:
1. Executive sponsorship (CIO/CXO level)
2. Shared language and vocabulary
3. Centralized knowledgebase for cross-mappings
4. Clear visualizations showing connections
5. Incremental approach (start small, prove value, scale)

For experience-led transformation:
1. Customer-centric culture
2. Integrated customer data
3. Agile, modular architecture
4. Design-IT collaboration
5. Metrics connecting experience to business outcomes

## Challenges Identified

**Integration Obstacles:**
- Organizational silos (different reporting structures)
- Maturity gaps between disciplines
- Terminology confusion and overlaps
- Tool fragmentation
- Unclear governance and decision rights

**Design Trade-offs:**
- Appropriate granularity for mappings
- Centralized vs distributed architectures
- Scope of CX team responsibilities
- Balance between vision and architectural constraints

## Emerging Trends

1. **AI-augmented architecture** - analyzing journey-capability relationships
2. **Real-time experience orchestration** - dynamic, individualized experiences at scale
3. **Ecosystem architectures** - extending beyond enterprise boundaries
4. **Continuous journey intelligence** - always-on measurement and adaptation
5. **Low-code experience composition** - business users assembling experiences from architectural building blocks
