## Research Summary

Tonight's research focused on the core principles of service design, particularly the influential five-principle framework established by Marc Stickdorn and Jakob Schneider in their 2011 book "This is Service Design Thinking."

### Key Findings

#### The Five Core Principles

1. **User-Centered (Human-Centered)**
   - Design services based on genuine understanding of ALL users (customers, employees, vendors)
   - Focus on users' perspectives, needs, behaviors, and psychological drivers
   - Design from outside-in, not inside-out

2. **Co-Creative**
   - Collaborative process involving all stakeholders in design
   - Ensures diverse voices and perspectives are heard
   - Creates better alignment, innovation, and acceptance

3. **Sequencing**
   - Visualize services as sequences of interrelated actions over time
   - Use service blueprints and customer journey maps
   - Identify pain points, opportunities, and critical touchpoints

4. **Evidencing**
   - Make intangible services tangible through physical/digital artifacts
   - Create memorable touchpoints (boarding passes, app interfaces, receipts)
   - Help customers understand and appreciate service value

5. **Holistic**
   - Consider the entire service ecosystem
   - Look beyond individual interactions to the complete experience
   - Account for cultural, technological, and economic factors

### Historical Context

- **1991:** Service design established as academic discipline by Michael Erlhoff and Birgit Mager at KISD
- **2009:** Stefan Moritz published foundational text on service design
- **2011:** Stickdorn & Schneider published "This is Service Design Thinking," establishing the five-principle framework
- **2010s-present:** Widespread adoption across commercial and public sectors

### Additional Frameworks Discovered

**Design4Services principles** extend beyond the core five to include:
- Process design principles (eliminate non-value-adding activities, minimize handovers)
- Organizational design principles (autonomy, competency-based organization)
- Information design principles (data normalization, reusability)
- Technology design principles (technology enables, not drives)

### Key Insight

> "When you have two coffee shops right next to each other, and each sells the exact same coffee at the exact same price, service design is what makes you walk into one and not the other."

This perfectly captures the essence of service design — it's not about the product itself, but the entire experience ecosystem surrounding it.

## Connection to Service Design × Business Architecture

These principles provide the foundational mindset for the intersection of service design and business architecture:

- **Holistic principle** connects directly to enterprise architecture's system thinking
- **Sequencing** aligns with value stream mapping and process architecture
- **Co-creative** principle mirrors stakeholder engagement in EA frameworks
- **User-centered** principle grounds business capability design in actual needs
- **Evidencing** provides tangible artifacts that can be mapped to business architecture components

The principles establish the human-centered, experience-focused lens that business architecture traditionally lacks, while business architecture provides the organizational structure and capability framework that service design requires for implementation at scale.
