## Research Summary

Tonight's research focused on **Journey Operations as an established organizational practice**—the maturation of customer journey management from design activity into continuous, enterprise-wide operating discipline.

### Key Insights Captured

1. **Journey Management Maturity Model (TheyDo)**
   - Documented 5 maturity levels: Intuition → Fragmented → Coordinated → Scalable → Excellence
   - Identified 6 critical dimensions: Governance, Process, Culture, Organization, Measurement, Tools
   - Key finding: Tools are only 1/6 of the equation; maturity requires parallel investment across all dimensions

2. **Journey Orchestration Framework (Pedowitz Group)**
   - Defined journey orchestration as "governed process of designing, sequencing, and optimizing interactions using data and rules in real time"
   - Documented shift from isolated campaigns to end-to-end journeys with signal-driven next steps
   - Captured 6-phase orchestration playbook: Design → Instrument → Orchestrate → Coordinate → Optimize → Govern
   - Detailed capability maturity matrix across journey framework, signals/scoring, cross-channel coordination, sales/CS alignment, measurement, and governance

3. **Modern Service Management Integration (Microsoft/ITSM.tools)**
   - Explored the 12-step journey to Modern Service Management
   - Documented integration points between ITSM and Journey Ops
   - Key principle: Legacy ITSM-heavy processes create bottlenecks; Journey Ops requires lightweight, autonomous release pipelines with embedded (not bolted-on) controls
   - Captured evolution of change management from approval gate to notification engine

4. **Journey Management Platform Landscape**
   - Researched 10+ platforms across categories: Mapping/Management (TheyDo, Smaply, UXPressia), Orchestration (Adobe, Genesys, SAP Emarsys), CX Data/Analytics (OpenText, Totango), Integration (Jira Service Mgmt, ServiceNow)
   - Documented selection criteria: open APIs, cross-silo data access, real-time capability, governance support

5. **Operating Rhythms & Roles**
   - Defined recurring cadences: Daily (health dashboards, incident response), Monthly (performance reviews, cross-functional syncs), Quarterly (strategy sessions, maturity assessments), Annual (roadmap planning, benchmarking)
   - Mapped role hierarchy from Chief Journey Officer → Journey Operations Manager → Journey Specialists
   - Created RACI matrix for journey optimization activities

6. **Measurement Framework**
   - Established three tiers of KPIs: Operational Health (uptime, MTTD/MTTR, data quality), Journey Performance (conversion, velocity, drop-offs), Business Impact (pipeline, revenue, CLV, NPS)
   - Added maturity advancement metrics to track progress across six dimensions

### Notable Practitioners & Sources

- **TheyDo**: Leading Journey Management Maturity Index framework and AI platform
- **Pedowitz Group**: Journey orchestration methodology for marketing/RevOps
- **Microsoft + ITSM.tools**: Modern Service Management 12-step journey (John Clark, Kathleen Wilson)
- **Prosci**: ADKAR change management model referenced for adoption

### Emerging Trends Identified

- AI agents increasingly planning, creating, and optimizing journeys autonomously
- Journey Ops platforms converging (mapping + orchestration + analytics + ITSM)
- Journey-as-Code: version-controlled, IaC-style management of journey definitions
- Predictive journey health: ML identifying at-risk journeys before metrics degrade
- Employee Journey Ops: HR/IT applying customer journey sophistication to workforce
